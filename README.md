# Module 4:- Email Etiquette and Basics of Social Media Etiquette
In this module we learn about the principles of professional communication in the digital world with the special emphasis on email and social media etiquette. It highlighted how proper communication practices contribute to building a positive personal and professional image especially in academic and workplace environments this importance of email etiquette was discussed in terms of clarity as emails are a primary mode of formal communication in organizations.

We were introduced to key guidelines of email etiquette such as using a clear and relevant subject line, maintaining a polite and formal tone, avoiding grammatical errors and being concise yet complete in communication 
The module also emphasized the need to avoid slang use appropriate greetings and signoffs and ensure that attachments and links are properly referenced additionally workplace etiquette was covered including timely responses, respectful communication with colleagues, maintaining confidentiality and using official channels appropriately.

A standard professional email structure includes:

**Subject Line**: Clearly states the purpose of the email<br>
**Salutation**: Formal greeting (e.g., Dear Sir/Madam, Dear [Name])<br>
**Opening Line**: Brief introduction or purpose of writing<br>
**Body**: Main content, presented clearly and logically<br>
**Closing Line**: Summary or call to action<br>
**Sign-off**: Polite closing (e.g., Regards, Sincerely)<br>
**Signature**: Name and relevant details (designation/contact information)<br>

This module also introduced us to the basics of social media etiquette such as focusing on responsible posting, respectful interactions, protecting personal information and maintaining a professional digital footprint.

# Module 5:- Awareness on Cyber Crime and Financial Fraud
In this module we learnt about various aspects of cybercrime and the importance of maintaining security in the digital environment
Cybercrime refers to illegal activities carried out using computers, networks or the internet, targeting individuals, organizations and financial systems. Different types of Cybercrime include hacking, phishing, identity theft, malware attacks and online scams explaining how attackers exploit both technical vulnerabilities and human behavior.

We also learnt about about different Cybercrimes in the financial domain including online banking fraud, credit/debit card fraud, OTP scams and fraudulent investment schemes also risks associated with Unified Payments Interface (UPI) which is widely used for instant digital transactions. While UPI is secure users can fall victim to frauds such as fake payment requests, QR code scams, phishing links, and unauthorized collect requests some prevention measures are verifying payment requests, not sharing UPI PINs and carefully checking transaction details before approving payments.

The module also discussed crimes at the personal level, including cyberstalking, cyberbullying, identity theft and misuse of personal data, emphasizing their impact on privacy and mental well-being. A cybercrime flowchart was introduced to explain the stages of a typical attack from target identification and data gathering to exploitation, execution and resulting damage , This helps students understand how such crimes are carried out.

Finally, cybercrime prevention measures were covered in detail, including using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding suspicious links and emails, regularly updating software and practicing safe online behavior. In the context of UPI and digital payments, users were advised to verify sources, avoid unknown QR codes and use trusted applications only. Overall, the module aimed to build awareness and equip individuals with practical knowledge to prevent and respond to cyber threats effectively.
